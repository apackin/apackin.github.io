Frequently Asked Questions

What is this all about?

The Lifer Club is an innovative approach to building a community that connects people to enjoy and learn from each other. Drawing inspiration from top country clubs, gated communities, or elite universities, The Lifer Club is a place where existing friendships can be strengthened and new friendships can be formed. But unlike a university where the goal is a degree, and unlike a country club where there are only a handful of core activities, and unlike a gated community where membership is limited by geographic proximity, The Lifer Club is exclusively focused on supporting a fulfilling and engaging lifestyle no matter what your passions are or where in the world you live.

What does a course consist of?

Every course has two parts; Part 1 is an interactive online seminar and Part 2 is an immersive destination-based capstone trip. In Part 1 the seminar leader facilitates an in-depth exploration of the course topic. Topics draw from the eclectic interests of our members including American civil war history, wines of Portugal, foods of India, plants and animals of the Amazon, photography of natural landscapes, East Asian religions and the Tour de France to name a few. Leveraging zoom video conferencing, the seminar is focused on fostering a lively discussion and whenever possible includes hands-on activities using “Travel Pack” items sent to participants at the start of the course. Part 1 gives members the chance to spend time together while building excitement for and enriching the capstone trip to come in Part 2. In Part 2 participants immerse themselves in the seminar topic by traveling to the destination of choice for a 7-14 day, often multi-city, trip. In stark opposition to the rigid “on the bus, off the bus” approach to group travel that is so common, each capstone trip has a cohesive theme that aligns to the class topic with certain tours and experiences booked for the entire group, but a wide range of suggested activities are left at the discretion of each participant if they want to partake or if they want to go off on their own path. It’s during these capstone trips that friendships initially formed virtually are solidified in real life.

What is a Travel Pack?

A Travel Pack is a box delivered to each participant at the start of a course that includes everything they’ll need to engage in the experiential hands-on portion of their seminar. Travel Pack items can range from key phrases language flashcards to premium bottles of bourbon or azuki red beans for a cooking class. Each Travel Pack is uniquely crafted by the seminar leader to enrich the course it serves.

How many members can enroll in a course?

Courses vary in size but typically range from 8-20 participants. Each member is allowed to invite one or two guests (often a spouse or child) to take part in the course as well. Ultimate course registration is at the discretion of the course leader.

Who is the ideal member?

The ideal “Lifer” is someone who has established themselves with a successful career that has given them the financial independence and flexibility to prioritize enjoying life. They are a lifelong learner, ready to explore their passions, interests, and the broader world around them. They are an independent thinker that willingly contributes to, and actively participates in, the communities they are a part of.

How is The Lifer Club funded?

Similar to a country club, membership consists of a one time initiation fee and annual dues with a minimum annual spend. The initiation fee is invested in developing new courses while the annual dues support general club operations.
